About Us

Cervical cancer is one of the most highly preventable cancers because there's a vaccine, the HPV vaccine, that prevents most of it. HPV is approximated to cause 70% of cervical cancer cases (WHO). Thus, providing access to the HPV vaccine is imperative. Additionally, cervical cancer is very treatable when caught in the early stages, so it is really important for women to get consistently screened so it can be caught early and treated successfully.

CCAP Across The Map, the acronym standing for cervical cancer awareness and prevention, is a nonprofit organization that works to decrease the global rate of cervical cancer through providing cervical health education and increasing access to HPV vaccines and diagnostic screening in under-resourced areas around the world.
